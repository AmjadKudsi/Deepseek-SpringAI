Certainly! The **water cycle**, also known as the **hydrologic cycle**, describes how water moves continuously through Earth's ecosystems. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown:

### 1. **Evaporation**  
   - The sun heats water in oceans, lakes, and rivers, turning it into **water vapor** (a gas).  
   - Plants also release water vapor through **transpiration** (a process called **evapotranspiration** when combined with evaporation).

### 2. **Condensation**  
   - As water vapor rises and cools in the atmosphere, it forms **clouds** (tiny water droplets or ice crystals).

### 3. **Precipitation**  
   - When clouds become heavy, water falls back to Earth as **rain, snow, sleet, or hail**.

### 4. **Runoff & Infiltration**  
   - Precipitation flows over the ground (**runoff**) into rivers, lakes, and oceans.  
   - Some water soaks into the soil (**infiltration**), replenishing groundwater.

### 5. **Collection**  
   - Water gathers in bodies of water (oceans, lakes), where the cycle repeats.

### **Key Notes**:  
- The cycle has no true "start" or "end"—it’s a continuous process.  
- Energy from the **sun** and Earth’s **gravity** drive the cycle.
